Around 1-am or so, I learned that being relatable to someone with no dev related skills and culturally different, or bouncing ideas with them, is very important.

Anyways, I showed him my apps, and soon after, he had an idea that could be implemented.

"Why can't we write an application that gives out the location of some party through Google Map (only with some special code),
but only show the details when the user arrives at the designated location?"

I thought to myself, "well, why don't you just show the users the details soon as the code has been received?"
He just thought people love s*** like AR, and they will blow money on anything that looks cool;
after some silence, I realized where he was getting at.

because of this, empty looking warehouse without any suspicion tends to be filled with ravers.

Some clubs had "exclusive features," provided only to the dedicated VIPs of their club (all of them he told me were horrible).
He was trying to tell me that location itself is the secondary password to access additional information about the party,
where the Cloud Anchor or Spatial Anchor object contains the details of it; after all, they can only be generated when they are at the actual location.

This idea doesn't have to be used for illegal stuff, but it could also be used for professors
hiding details about additional class room meetups, or conference administrators hiding details
about special dinner invites (Facebook was handing out physical invitations to CppCon attendees, where one could dig through the garbage can and get accepted, which I kinda did ...)
I am gonna try to implement something like the above during this year's Local Hack Day.
Maybe, lesson I've learned from this meeting with him is that developers are here for the folks with no dev skills,
